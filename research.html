Research

My research focuses on advanced manufacturing, additive manufacturing, powder processing, materials characterization, and data-driven process improvement. I use experimental design and statistical analysis to understand the relationships among process parameters, powder characteristics, and manufactured part quality.

Binder Jetting Additive Manufacturing

My current research investigates binder jetting additive manufacturing using ceramic, biomass-derived, biowaste-derived, and composite feedstock powders.

Materials investigated include alumina, silicon carbide, boron nitride, zeolites, algae powder, eggshell-derived powder, and composite feedstocks.

  • Effects of powder packing rate on dimensional accuracy, structural integrity, and green-part quality
  • Effects of layer thickness, compaction thickness, binder saturation, powder dispensing conditions, roller speed, and temperature
  • Binder–powder compatibility and powder-bed behavior
  • Powder segregation during mixing and spreading
  • Operation and troubleshooting of the ExOne Innovent+ and ComeTrue T10 binder jetting systems

Powder Granulation and Feedstock Development

I develop granulated feedstock powders for additive manufacturing using spray freeze drying.

My work examines how granulation process variables influence granule size, shape, flowability, and suitability for powder-bed processing.

  • Granulation of fine eggshell powder and alumina powder
  • Effects of atomization pressure, slurry feed rate, and solid loading
  • Development of sustainable and waste-derived feedstock materials
  • Evaluation of granulated powders for binder jetting additive manufacturing

Powder Characterization

I characterize feedstock powders to evaluate their particle size, packing behavior, and flowability.

  • Particle size distribution using laser scattering analysis
  • Apparent density and tap density measurements
  • Angle of repose and powder flowability evaluation
  • Hall flowmeter and tap density testing
  • Evaluation of binder–powder interactions

Materials Characterization and Part Evaluation

I use experimental and analytical methods to evaluate manufactured parts and powder-based materials.

  • Archimedes' density measurement
  • X-ray diffraction
  • Scanning electron microscopy
  • Energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy
  • BET surface-area analysis
  • Mechanical and compressive-strength testing
  • Porosity and microstructure evaluation
  • Surface finishing through grinding and lapping

Design of Experiments and Statistical Analysis

I use structured experimentation and statistical methods to identify significant process variables and improve manufacturing consistency.

  • Factorial experimental designs
  • Randomization and blocking
  • Analysis of variance
  • t-tests and F-tests
  • Paired comparisons
  • Process variability and dimensional accuracy analysis

Bioprinting and Biofabrication

My previous research at The University of Texas Rio Grande Valley involved electrohydrodynamic printing, laser-induced forward transfer, and co-axial extrusion bioprinting.

  • Pulse-electric-field-assisted electrohydrodynamic printing of sodium alginate
  • Laser-induced forward transfer of collagen I with retinal photoreceptor cells
  • Cell viability and morphology evaluation in bioprinted constructs
  • Development of co-axial extrusion systems for hollow microvessels
  • Evaluation of bioreactor designs for vascular tissue culture
